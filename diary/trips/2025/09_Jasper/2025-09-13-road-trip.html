Johnson Lake & Kinney Lake

Every great adventure starts long before the first photograph is taken. For us, it began on a Thursday evening with a packed vehicle, mountain bikes, paddleboards, and the excitement of finally escaping the city. Our destination was the Canadian Rockies, but we weren't in a hurry. The road itself was part of the adventure.

The Journey North

After work we headed west, watching the landscape slowly transform from rolling hills into towering mountain ranges. Before reaching our overnight stop, we made time to visit Helmcken Falls along with a couple of nearby waterfalls in Wells Gray Provincial Park. Hearing the roar of the waterfalls in the cool evening air felt like the official beginning of our trip.

By the time we rolled into Valemount later that night, everyone was tired—but excited for what tomorrow had in store.

Day Highlights

  • 🚗 Evening drive into the mountains
  • 🌊 Helmcken Falls & nearby waterfalls
  • 🏨 Overnight stay in Valemount

Crystal Waters of Johnson Lake

The next morning greeted us with sunshine and one of the most beautiful lakes we've ever seen. Johnson Lake almost didn't look real. The water shimmered in brilliant shades of turquoise, surrounded by dense forests that reflected perfectly across its calm surface.

My fiancée and I wasted no time unloading the paddleboards. Within minutes we were gliding across unbelievably clear water while soaking in every direction we looked. It was peaceful, quiet, and one of those rare moments where time seems to slow down.

The clearest water we'd paddled on all year.
While we explored the lake, Dad stayed behind preparing lunch for everyone. Talk about perfect timing.
"The best memories aren't always made at famous destinations. Sometimes they're made floating quietly on a lake with the people you love."

Trading Paddleboards for Pedals

After another restful night in Valemount, it was time to switch gears—literally. The paddleboards stayed behind as the mountain bikes came out. Our destination was Kinney Lake, one of the first major highlights along the famous Berg Lake Trail beneath Mount Robson.

The ride wasn't overly technical, but it was long enough to earn every view. Each climb rewarded us with rushing rivers, towering evergreens, and glimpses of glaciers hidden among the mountains.

The ride toward Kinney Lake was every bit as scenic as we'd hoped.
Reaching Kinney Lake made every pedal stroke worth it.

Standing beside the glacier-fed lake with Mount Robson towering above us was one of those moments that photographs can only partially capture. We spent a while simply taking it all in before grabbing a few victory photos and beginning the ride back.

Looking Back

It's funny how quickly road trips begin to blur together, but this day has always stood out. Between the waterfalls, paddleboarding on Johnson Lake, sharing lunch with Dad, and biking to Kinney Lake, it perfectly captured what we wanted this trip to be about.

Not rushing from attraction to attraction, but slowing down enough to enjoy every stop along the way.

By the end of the day we had already crossed off several bucket-list locations, yet it somehow felt like the adventure was only just beginning.
