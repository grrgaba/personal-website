About

Short about page — simple and to the point.

Hi, I'm Nico.

I built this website because I needed a place to collect all the things that constantly compete for space in my head: travel plans, expedition notes, gear reviews, random projects, lessons learned, useful tools, and ideas that seemed too interesting to throw away.

I have always been fascinated by two very different worlds.

The first is technology. I love understanding how things work, taking systems apart, solving problems, and building things that didn't exist yesterday. Whether it's a tiny gadget, a piece of software, or some ridiculous side project that started as a shower thought, I enjoy the process of turning ideas into something real.

The second is exploration.

Mountains. Glaciers. Volcanoes. Remote trails. Long road trips. Places where cell service disappears and the scenery reminds you how small you actually are. Some people relax at a resort. I usually find myself looking at maps, weather forecasts, hiking routes, and wondering what's hiding beyond the next ridge.

What interests me most is the overlap between adventure and curiosity. Every trip becomes a research project. Every problem becomes a puzzle. Every destination turns into a story. I like understanding not only where I am, but how it got there, who discovered it, what makes it unique, and what most people miss when they visit.

I'm not particularly interested in luxury travel, collecting trophies, or pretending to be an influencer. I'd rather have an unforgettable experience, a few great photos, and a ridiculous story than the perfect social media post.

This website is my personal corner of the internet where I document those experiences. Some pages are useful. Some are nerdy. Some are probably only interesting to me. That's okay.

You'll find travel itineraries, expedition planning notes, gear thoughts, technical experiments, project writeups, and occasionally evidence that curiosity can be both a blessing and a terrible way to spend an entire weekend.

If there's a common theme behind everything here, it's simple:

Life is more interesting when you keep learning, keep building, and keep exploring.

Current status: probably planning the next trip, testing a new gadget, or trying to solve a problem that absolutely did not need to become a three-hour project.
